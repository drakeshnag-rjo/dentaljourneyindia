All-on-4 is a revolutionary dental implant technique that replaces an entire arch of teeth using just four strategically placed implants. It's the most sought-after solution for patients with multiple missing teeth, failing dentitions, or those tired of dealing with dentures. What Is All-on-4?

The All-on-4 concept, pioneered by Nobel Biocare, uses four dental implants to support a full arch of fixed teeth. Two implants are placed vertically at the front of the jaw, and two are placed at an angle (up to 45°) at the back. This angled placement achieves maximum bone contact without needing bone grafting — even in patients with significant bone loss.

The result is a fixed, non-removable set of teeth that look, feel, and function like natural teeth. Unlike dentures, they don't slip, don't need adhesive, and allow you to eat anything — including hard foods like apples and nuts.

All-on-4 vs All-on-6

All-on-6 uses six implants instead of four, providing extra stability. It's recommended for patients with good bone density who want maximum strength, larger jaws where four implants may not provide sufficient support, and patients who prefer the added security of two extra anchor points. The choice between 4 and 6 implants depends on your bone density and jaw anatomy — your implantologist will recommend the best option after a CBCT scan.

The All-on-4 Process in India

Day 1 — Comprehensive Assessment: Full clinical examination, CBCT scan (3D imaging), digital smile design, and treatment plan confirmation. If extractions are needed, they may begin today.

Day 2 — Surgery Day: All remaining teeth (if any) are extracted, four implants are placed, and a temporary fixed bridge is attached — all in one session under local anesthesia or sedation. You walk out with a full set of teeth. The procedure takes 2–4 hours per arch.

Days 3–7 — Recovery: Soft diet for the first few days. Most patients experience mild swelling and discomfort managed with prescribed medication. Follow-up appointments on days 3 and 7 for checks and adjustments. Between appointments, you're free to explore the city.

3–6 Months Later — Final Bridge: After the implants fully integrate with your jawbone (osseointegration), you return for 3–5 days to have your permanent zirconia or acrylic bridge fitted. Some patients choose to have the temporary bridge made to a high standard and delay the permanent bridge to a convenient time.

Who's a Good Candidate?

  • Patients missing most or all teeth in one or both arches
  • Those with failing teeth that need full extraction
  • Current denture wearers who want a permanent, fixed solution
  • Patients with moderate bone loss (the angled implants often eliminate the need for bone grafting)
  • Patients in good general health who can undergo minor oral surgery

Choosing a Clinic for All-on-4 in India

All-on-4 is an advanced surgical procedure — choosing the right clinic is critical. Look for an implantologist with specific All-on-4 training and hundreds (ideally thousands) of cases, a clinic with in-house CBCT scanning and a dental lab for same-day prosthetics, clinics that use reputable implant systems (Nobel Biocare's original All-on-4 protocol, Straumann Pro Arch, or equivalent), and transparent pricing that includes implants, temporary bridge, all follow-ups, and the permanent bridge.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do I need to stay in India for All-on-4?

Plan for 7–10 days for the initial surgery and temporary teeth. You'll need to return for 3–5 days after 3–6 months for the permanent bridge. Some patients combine the second visit with a vacation.

Is All-on-4 painful?

The procedure is performed under local anesthesia or IV sedation — you won't feel pain during surgery. Post-operative discomfort is manageable with prescribed pain medication and typically subsides within 3–5 days.

How long do All-on-4 implants last?

The implants themselves can last a lifetime with proper care. The prosthetic bridge typically lasts 15–25 years for zirconia and 10–15 years for acrylic before needing replacement.

Can I fly home after surgery?

Most patients fly home 5–7 days after surgery without issues. Your clinic will confirm you're safe to travel before you leave.
