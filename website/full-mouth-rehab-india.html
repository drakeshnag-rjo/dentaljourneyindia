Full Mouth Rehabilitation in India: Costs, Clinics & What to Expect

A complete guide for patients needing extensive dental work — multiple implants, crowns, bridges, and full smile restoration at a fraction of Western prices.

Updated April 2026 · 7 min read

Full mouth rehabilitation — also called full mouth reconstruction or full mouth restoration — is a comprehensive treatment plan that addresses all the teeth in both arches. It's designed for patients with extensive dental problems: multiple missing teeth, severe decay, worn-down teeth, bite issues, or a combination of these.

In the US, full mouth rehab can cost $30,000–$60,000 or more. In India, the same comprehensive treatment costs $4,000–$10,000 — using the same materials and techniques.

Key insight: Full mouth rehab is where dental tourism makes the most financial sense. The savings of $20,000–$50,000 easily cover multiple trips to India, premium hotels, and a vacation — with money left over.

What Does Full Mouth Rehab Include?

Every full mouth rehab is unique — it's tailored to your specific dental condition. A typical plan may include some or all of the following: extraction of damaged or non-restorable teeth, dental implants to replace missing teeth, root canal treatment for teeth that can be saved, crowns to restore damaged teeth, bridges to replace gaps, gum treatment if periodontal disease is present, bite alignment (occlusal adjustment), and a final prosthetic plan for optimal aesthetics and function.

Treatment Timeline

Full mouth rehab is typically completed in two trips to India:

Trip 1 (7–14 days): Comprehensive examination and 3D imaging, treatment planning and digital smile design, extractions (if needed), implant placement, root canals, bone grafting (if needed), temporary restorations so you leave with functional teeth.

Trip 2 (5–7 days, after 3–6 months): Final impressions after implants have integrated, permanent crown and bridge fitting, bite adjustment and final polishing, post-treatment review and care instructions.

Some clinics can complete simpler cases in a single extended trip of 2–3 weeks, especially if no implants are needed.

Who Needs Full Mouth Rehab?

  • Patients with multiple missing teeth across both arches
  • Those with severely worn, cracked, or decayed teeth
  • Patients who've avoided dentistry for years and need comprehensive treatment
  • People with bite problems (TMJ issues, uneven wear)
  • Those who want to replace old, failing dental work (old crowns, bridges, fillings)
  • Denture wearers who want a permanent, fixed solution

Why India Is Ideal for Full Mouth Rehab

Full mouth rehabilitation is complex work — it requires a team of specialists working together. India's top dental centers have the multi-disciplinary teams needed: implantologists, prosthodontists, endodontists, periodontists, and orthodontists all under one roof. In-house dental labs mean faster turnaround on crowns and prosthetics, and the cost savings on a $30,000+ treatment plan are life-changing.
